❓ Frequently Asked Questions

How does the dog breed match quiz work?
Our algorithm matches your lifestyle answers against 30 popular dog breeds across 8 key factors: living space, activity level, family situation, experience, shedding tolerance, size preference, grooming commitment, and climate. Each breed is scored based on how well its traits align with your answers, and we show your top matches ranked by compatibility percentage.
What's the best dog breed for apartments?
Small to medium breeds with lower energy levels tend to do best in apartments. French Bulldogs, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, Bichon Frises, and Shih Tzus are excellent apartment dogs. Some medium breeds like Corgis can also adapt well if they get enough exercise. Take our quiz above for personalized recommendations based on your specific situation.
What's the best family dog breed?
Labrador Retrievers and Golden Retrievers are classic family dogs for good reason — they're patient, gentle, and great with kids. Other excellent family breeds include Beagles, Boxers, and Cavalier King Charles Spaniels. The best family dog also depends on your children's ages, your activity level, and your living space. Our quiz considers all these factors.
What's the best dog breed for first-time owners?
Breeds that are eager to please, moderately energetic, and forgiving of training mistakes are ideal for first-timers. Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Cavalier King Charles Spaniels, Poodles, Bichon Frises, and Havanese are all excellent choices. We generally recommend avoiding very independent or high-energy breeds like Huskies, German Shepherds, or Border Collies for complete beginners.
How accurate is this quiz?
This quiz provides a great starting point based on general breed traits, but every dog is an individual. We recommend using your top matches as a "short list" to research further — talk to breeders, rescue organizations, and current owners of those breeds. Always consider adopting from a shelter or rescue where mixed-breed dogs can make wonderful companions too.
Should I get a purebred or a mixed breed?
Both have advantages. Purebreds offer predictable size, temperament, and coat type, which is helpful if you have specific needs. Mixed breeds often have fewer genetic health problems and are wonderfully unique. Adoption from a shelter is a fantastic option — about 30% of shelter dogs are purebreds.
