❓ Dog Ear Care FAQs

How often should I clean my dog's ears?
Most dogs with healthy ears need cleaning once a month. Dogs prone to ear infections or with floppy ears (like Cocker Spaniels, Labradors, and Basset Hounds) may need weekly cleaning. Dogs with chronic ear issues should follow your vet's recommended schedule. Over-cleaning can cause irritation, so only clean when needed. Use our checker above to assess when it's time.
What are signs of a dog ear infection?
Common signs include: redness or swelling inside the ear, yellow or bloody discharge, a strong unpleasant odor, excessive head shaking or scratching, tilting the head to one side, sensitivity when the ears are touched, and crusting or scabs on the ear flap. If you notice any of these, especially discharge or odor, schedule a vet visit promptly. Shop ear infection treatments →
Can I use Q-tips to clean my dog's ears?
No — never use Q-tips or cotton swabs inside your dog's ear canal. They can push wax and debris deeper into the ear, cause impaction, or even rupture the eardrum. Instead, use a vet-recommended ear cleaning solution and cotton balls or gauze pads. Only clean what you can see — the outer ear and the inside of the ear flap. Never insert anything into the vertical ear canal.
What causes dog ear infections?
Ear infections in dogs are most commonly caused by bacteria or yeast (Malassezia). Contributing factors include: moisture trapped in the ear (common in swimmers and floppy-eared breeds), allergies (food or environmental), excess moisture or humidity, foreign bodies like grass awns, hormonal imbalances, and autoimmune conditions. Identifying and addressing the underlying cause is key to preventing recurring infections.
Are certain dog breeds more prone to ear problems?
Yes. Dogs with floppy or hairy ears tend to have more ear issues because airflow is restricted, creating a warm, moist environment where yeast and bacteria thrive. Breeds at higher risk include: Cocker Spaniels, Labrador Retrievers, Golden Retrievers, Basset Hounds, Poodles, Shih Tzus, and Bulldogs. Dogs that swim frequently are also more susceptible. Regular ear checks and cleaning are especially important for these breeds.
What's the best way to dry my dog's ears after swimming or bathing?
After swimming or bathing, gently dry the outer ear and ear flap with a soft towel. You can use a vet-recommended drying solution or ear powder to help absorb moisture in the ear canal. Avoid getting water directly into your dog's ears during baths — place a cotton ball loosely in each ear (remove afterward).
