      ❓ Frequently Asked Questions

      How much does a dog cost in the first year?
      For a medium-sized dog with moderate spending, first-year costs typically range from $1,500 to $4,000. This includes one-time expenses (adoption fee, spay/neuter, initial supplies) plus the first year of food, vet care, and insurance. Our calculator breaks this down based on your specific choices.
      How much does a cat cost per year?
      Cats are generally more affordable than dogs. First-year costs for a cat range from $800 to $2,500, with annual recurring costs of about $500 to $1,500. The biggest savings come from smaller food portions, no grooming costs, and lower insurance premiums.
      What's the most expensive part of owning a pet?
      For most pet owners, food is the largest recurring expense — up to $1,500/year for large breed dogs. However, medical emergencies are the biggest financial risk. A single surgery or emergency visit can cost $2,000–$10,000, which is why pet insurance is strongly recommended. Compare pet insurance plans →
      Does pet size really affect the cost?
      Absolutely. A Giant breed dog can cost 2–4× more per year than a small breed. Food scales with size: a 5 kg dog eats ~150 g/day while a 50 kg dog eats ~500 g/day. Larger dogs also have higher insurance premiums, require more medication, and often have shorter lifespans. Check our calculator by selecting different sizes to see the difference.
      Is pet insurance worth the cost?
      Pet insurance costs ~$25–70/month for dogs and ~$15–40/month for cats. While it feels like an extra expense, a single emergency can cost thousands. Insurance is most valuable for unexpected accidents and illnesses — cancer treatment, broken bones, or emergency surgery. Consider it budget protection rather than a routine expense. Read our full insurance guide →
      What are the hidden costs of pet ownership?
      First-time owners often underestimate: (1) Emergency vet visits — the #1 budget breaker. (2) Pet deposits/rent for apartments. (3) Boarding or pet sitters when traveling. (4) Dental care — cleanings cost $200–600. (5) End-of-life care. We recommend setting aside $500–1,000 as a pet emergency fund from day one.
      How can I save money on pet care?
      Smart savings strategies: (1) Buy food in bulk or subscribe for auto-ship discounts. (2) Get pet insurance while your pet is young and healthy. (3) DIY grooming for simple breeds. (4) Preventive care is cheaper than treatment — regular vet checkups catch issues early. (5) Adopt from a shelter rather than buying from a breeder to save hundreds on the initial fee. Shop auto-ship deals →
      How much should I budget per month for a dog?
      On average, budget $100–250/month for a dog including food ($30–80), treats ($10–20), routine vet ($15–35), grooming ($15–40), insurance ($25–60), and toys/supplies ($10–20). The first year is higher due to one-time setup costs. Use our calculator above to get a personalized monthly estimate.
